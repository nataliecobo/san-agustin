---
title: "Unpicking Colonial Narratives: Creating the myth of the Spanish conquest of the Philippines"
---

This is an emblemmatic image of the aspirations of the early modern Spanish empire. The gigantic figures of Augustinian missionaries on one side and _conquistadores_ on the other, march across the Philippine archipelago to meet one another in the middle, leaving the impression that the lands beneath their feet have been subsumed into a Catholic, Iberian imperial project. The symbol representing the Holy Name of Jesus shines on the entire enterprise, suggesting, at the very least, divine approval of these efforts. Along with the title of the printed text this image accompanies (_Conquistas de las Islas Philipinas_), this picture presents a story of harmonious, uncontested, and complete conquest. We do not have to scracth the surface of this image very deeply to see the false premises on which this myth was made. Let's take a closer look at what we are seeing.
